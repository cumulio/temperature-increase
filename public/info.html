The Data

The data in the ‘Global Temperature Rise’ dashboard shows surface air temperature changes (⁰C) measured over the global land area during the period 1961-2020 for 190 countries and 38 territories. This data is made publicly available by the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations.

The full documentation on this data can be found in the Temperature change statistics 1961–2020 (Faostat Analytical Brief 19), and were produced in collaboration with the NASA Goddard Institute for Space Studies

Temperature change is calculated over a baseline ‘climate norm’ corresponding to the period 1951–1980.

How to use the dashboard

The dashboard will initialize for the date range 2010-2020. You can either select subregions, or for more accurate data, deselect all countries & areas first, and pick out explicitly the countries or areas you are interested in.

At the bottom you will see 2 maps next to each other. These maps display average temperature rises for Northern Hemisphere Summer time and winter time respectively. Use this to see some surprising results. For example, this shows us that while Europe has seen warmer summers, Northern America has on average had a higher increase in temperature in the winters.

Insights

In 2020, global mean annual temperature change over land was the highest in the instrumental record, 1.71 ⁰C (Select 'World' from the Area picker) above the 1951–1980 climate normal.

In 2020, the mean annual temperature change was largest in Europe (3.3 ⁰C), followed by Asia (1.5 ⁰C), Oceania and South America (1.3 ⁰C), Africa and North America (1.2 ⁰C).
